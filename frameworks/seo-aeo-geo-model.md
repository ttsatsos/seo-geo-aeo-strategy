# SEO, AEO, and GEO Model

## SEO: Discoverability

SEO makes content findable and rankable in traditional search systems.

Important signals:

- crawlability and indexation
- page speed and technical health
- keyword targeting and search intent match
- title tags, meta descriptions, headings, and internal links
- content depth and topical authority
- structured data
- backlinks and brand authority

Failure mode: AI and search systems cannot reliably find the page.

## AEO: Extractability

AEO makes content easy for answer engines to parse and reuse.

Important signals:

- concise answer blocks near the top of the page
- question-led sections
- clear definitions
- comparison tables
- pros and cons
- FAQs
- how-to steps
- schema markup
- unambiguous entity names

Failure mode: systems can read the page but cannot easily turn it into a direct answer.

## GEO: Recommendability

GEO makes the brand, product, or page likely to be cited or recommended by generative engines.

Important signals:

- complete product and entity data
- consistent brand mentions across the web
- third-party references, reviews, awards, and backlinks
- original data or category expertise
- fresh prices, availability, specs, and product feeds
- comparison pages and decision guides
- evidence-backed claims
- clear founder, author, or expert identity

Failure mode: systems find the brand but favor better-cited or more verifiable competitors.

## Unified Page Standard

A page should be:

- **Complete:** covers the buyer's real decision criteria.
- **Identifiable:** names products, categories, brand, location, people, and use cases clearly.
- **Trusted:** provides evidence, reviews, citations, authorship, and external validation.
- **Extractable:** uses structured sections, tables, FAQs, schema, and concise answer text.
