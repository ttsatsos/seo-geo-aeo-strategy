# Ecommerce GEO Checklist

This checklist is adapted from saved ecommerce GEO notes and should be used for product, collection, comparison, and buying-guide pages.

## 1. Product-First Data

Every product should expose complete, current, machine-readable data:

- product name
- brand
- category
- size or dimensions
- material or ingredients
- use case
- price
- availability
- variants
- images
- reviews
- shipping/gifting constraints

## 2. CITE Framework

Content should be:

- **Complete:** enough detail to answer buyer questions.
- **Identifiable:** clear names, SKUs, brand, category, and entity relationships.
- **Trusted:** reviews, mentions, backlinks, awards, author/founder context, sources.
- **Extractable:** schema, tables, FAQs, concise answers, clean headings.

## 3. PDP Optimization

The product detail page is the AI storefront.

Each PDP should include:

- a direct product summary
- buyer-use-case language
- clear specs
- differentiation vs alternatives
- review highlights
- FAQ block
- structured data
- internal links to guides and comparisons

## 4. Author and Founder Authority

Connect brand claims to real people where appropriate.

Useful signals:

- founder page
- author bios
- expert reviewers
- interviews
- awards
- press mentions
- LinkedIn and organization consistency

## 5. Fresh Catalog Data

Keep product data fresh across the site, feeds, and third-party surfaces.

Critical fields:

- price
- inventory
- shipping deadlines
- variant availability
- specs
- product status
- seasonal gifting windows

## 6. Multi-Format Content

LLMs and answer engines benefit from multiple structured content formats:

- FAQs
- comparison tables
- ingredient/material pages
- buyer guides
- "best for" lists
- price-tier guides
- review roundups
- decision trees

## 7. Off-Site Trust

Build consistent brand signals outside the owned site.

Targets:

- reputable press
- industry directories
- review platforms
- partner pages
- social profiles
- marketplace profiles
- local/business listings
- relevant backlinks

## 8. Original Research

Publish category data that competitors do not have.

Examples:

- buyer preference surveys
- usage data
- ingredient/material explainers
- pricing benchmarks
- installation cost ranges
- seasonal gifting trends
- customer review analysis

## 9. GEO Audit

Test real buyer prompts in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI surfaces.

Track:

- whether the brand appears
- whether products appear
- exact recommendation language
- cited sources
- competitors mentioned
- missing trust signals
- content gaps

## 10. AI Share of Voice

Monitor mention share against competitors over a fixed prompt set.

Metrics:

- brand mentioned
- brand cited
- product recommended
- ranking/order in answer
- citation source
- competitor overlap
- answer sentiment
- reason for recommendation
