# Comparison Page Strategy

Comparison pages are a priority format for SEO, AEO, and GEO because they match buyer intent and are easy for AI systems to extract.

## Core Formats

1. **X vs Y Head-to-Head**
   Compare two products, categories, materials, or approaches directly.

2. **Best X for Use Case**
   Recommend products by buyer scenario, constraint, or job to be done.

3. **Feature, Spec, Ingredient, or Material Matrix**
   Use structured tables to compare attributes side by side.

4. **Price-Tier Comparison**
   Segment products by budget, mid-range, premium, or project size.

5. **When to Choose A Over B**
   Use conditional decision rules: if X, choose A; if Y, choose B.

6. **Customer Review Roundup**
   Aggregate real review language into themes, pros, cons, and use cases.

## Recommended Page Structure

1. Direct answer summary.
2. Best choice table.
3. Side-by-side comparison matrix.
4. Use-case recommendations.
5. Pros and cons.
6. Price or budget guidance.
7. Evidence and review themes.
8. FAQs.
9. Product/category links.
10. Schema markup.

## Quality Bar

A comparison page must be useful to a buyer, not just attractive to crawlers.

Good pages include:

- specific entities
- concrete specs
- transparent tradeoffs
- real buyer scenarios
- price context
- current products
- concise answer blocks
- internal links
- evidence
